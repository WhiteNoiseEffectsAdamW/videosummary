April 4, 2026
Follow-up to design-assessment.txt and design-session-summary.txt

================================================================================
OG IMAGE DECISION
================================================================================

CHOSEN: L2 (primary) + D2 (product shares)

L2 — Cream gradient background (#fdf8ef → #f5ede0), 4px amber left stripe at
50% opacity, EB Garamond 108px wordmark, Inter 28px uppercase tagline with 4px
letter-spacing, 4% noise. Left-aligned.

D2 — Near-black (#0a0a0a), amber top bar gradient fading right, EB Garamond
112px wordmark, Inter 34px light uppercase tagline with 2px letter-spacing,
5.5% noise. Left-aligned.

ROUTING:
  L2 → marketing pages (landing, pricing, about)
  D2 → shared product pages (/summary/:id, any user-shared link)

This extends the light-landing/dark-product split to social cards. Two images,
same typographic hierarchy (Garamond name, Inter tagline), different contexts.

WHY L2 WINS OVERALL:
- Garamond wordmark matches the product brand (no disconnect on tap-through)
- Cream background stands out in social feeds full of white and dark cards
- Left stripe adds editorial structure (margin rule, not decoration)
- Left-aligned holds at thumbnail size (~300px in iMessage) — reading direction
  anchors to a consistent left edge

WHY LEFT-ALIGNED, NOT CENTERED:
- Editorial, not ceremonial. Left alignment reads as a column/masthead.
  Centered reads as a title card or invitation — wrong personality.
- The left stripe depends on it. Centered text turns the stripe into a random
  decorative line. Left-aligned, it's a typographic margin that organizes the
  composition.
- At thumbnail size, centered text creates a floating effect with no anchor
  point. Left-aligned gives the eye a fixed starting edge.

REJECTED VARIANTS AND WHY:
- D1 (Inter bold): Generic. Every YC startup ships this OG image.
- D7, L3, L6 (Playfair Display): Introduces a third typeface not in the brand
  system. Playfair at 900 weight is loud and decorative — different personality
  than Headwater's restraint. Don't add a font for one asset.
- D6, L4 (label + italic stacked): Inverts the hierarchy — brand name becomes
  a small label, tagline becomes dominant. Wrong for OG images where you need
  people to learn the name at a glance.
- L5 (Inter bold wordmark): Inter 800 wordmark doesn't match the EB Garamond
  wordmark in the actual product. Brand dissonance on tap-through.
- L2b (0.08 noise): At thumbnail size, heavy noise risks looking like JPEG
  compression artifacts. 0.04 is subliminal, which is correct.

================================================================================
LANDING PAGE BACKGROUND DECISION
================================================================================

CHOSEN: Cream gradient + 4% noise from V1, color system from V0.

BACKGROUND:
  linear-gradient(160deg, #fdf8ef 0%, #f5ede0 100%)
  4% fractalNoise overlay

COLOR SYSTEM (kept from V0 — NOT V1's amber-tinted grays):
  Body text:     #777 (neutral gray, not #6b5a3a)
  Nav links:     #999 (neutral gray, not #9a7d50)
  Footer text:   #aaa (neutral gray, not #a08a5e)
  Borders:       #e8e4dc (neutral, not rgba(180,140,60,0.18))
  Card surfaces: #fff opaque (not rgba(255,255,255,0.6))
  Card labels:   #bbb (neutral, not #b09060)

WHY THE GRADIENT + NOISE:
- Addresses the "harder to make feel premium" concern from the original
  assessment. Flat #fafaf7 is clean but inert. The diagonal gradient adds
  depth — "paper surface with light falling across it" rather than "white page
  with warm tint."
- 4% noise creates the impression of a physical surface (paper, linen) without
  being identifiable as a filter. Subliminal texture.

WHY NOT V1'S FULL AMBER TINTING:
- When every secondary element is amber-brown, the amber accent (#b8924a)
  stops being an accent. It becomes the palette's undertone. The eyebrow
  "THE VIDEO, NOT THE TITLE" no longer pops against neutral grays — it's just
  a slightly more saturated version of the brown that's everywhere.
- Semi-transparent card backgrounds (rgba 255,255,255,0.6) let the gradient
  bleed through, making card surfaces shift with scroll position. That's
  visual instability, not texture.
- The hierarchy flattens. V0's neutral grays create a clear separation between
  "brand color" (amber) and "structural color" (gray). V1 collapses that.

WHY NOT V2 (7.5% NOISE):
- At 7.5% the noise becomes a visible grain layer, especially on the gradient.
  Scrolling a full page, your eye catches the pattern repeating. It reads as
  "Instagram filter" rather than "paper texture." The point of noise is that
  you shouldn't be able to point at it.
